    • Supporters

    Supporters are an essential part of a deck. You can only use one supporter per turn, and you can't use a supporter on you first turn. Some supporters give you and your opponent a fresh hand, some let you get your eveolution Pokemon(more on that later,), and others make your opponent switch their active Pokemon. You have to make sure your supporters align with the tactics of your deck. So if your deck was about stalling like the Snorlax Stall deck, you could use a Boss's Orders to put up your opponent's Pokemon with the highest retreat cost. Or if you want to speed things up with your evolutions, you can use a suporter like Jacq to take two evolutuion Pokemon out of your deck.

    How to play

    To start the game off, you shuffle your sixty card deck and hand it to your opponent. Your opponent with either cut or tap it. If your opponent choses to shuffle your deck, you get the final cut. Then you draw seven cards and look at them. If you have a basic pokemon in your hand, put it down in the middle of your play space. IF you have any more basic pokemon, you can put them in your bench below you active pokemon. You can only have five pokemon on your bench at a time. Then, you draw six cards and you DO NOT look at them. You put them face down on the top left space of your play area. Then put your deck face down in the top right of your play area. After setup, roll a dice or flip a coin. If you called heads and it was heads or an even number, you go first. To start your turn, you draw one card. You can attach one energy to your pokemon once per turn, play as many item cards as you want to, attach one tool per pokemon, put down a stadium, use stadiums or abilities, or play supporters. If you are going first, you CANNOT play a supporter until your next turn. You can only play one supporter per turn. At the end of your turn, you can attack. Make sure you read your cards. If you want to attack, make sure you have the right amouint of energy on your pokemon. It will say on the left of the attack you want to use. When you attack, you take one prize card if it's a normal pokemon, but you take two if it's a pokemon EX. To win, you can one, take all of your prize cards, two, run your opponent out of deck, or three, kill your opponents active pokemon and he has no bench pokemon. If you get knocked out, you promote one of your benched pokemon to the active spot. If you want to evolve a pokemon, your pokemon has to have been in last turn. If it wasn't, you cannot evole it.
